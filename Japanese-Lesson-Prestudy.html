Introductions & Occupations

WHAT YOU WILL LEARN
In this lesson, you will learn how to:
• Introduce yourself (name and occupation)
• Describe other people
• Say where someone is from
• Use the word ですdesu ("am / is / are")

WHAT IS ですdesu?

English Explanation

ですdesu is one of the most important words in Japanese. It works like "am," "is," or "are" in English.

Unlike English, ですdesu always comes at the end of the sentence.

It does not change form — whether you say "I am," "she is," or "they are," the word is always ですdesu.

HOW JAPANESE SENTENCES WORK

Word Order

English: I am a student.

Japanese: わたしwatashi はwa がくせいgakusei ですdesu。

The particle はwa marks the topic — it tells the listener who or what you are talking about. Think of it as "As for ___."

Note: はwa is written with the character "ha" but pronounced "wa" when used as a particle.

PATTERN 1: IDENTITY

"I am a ___."

Pattern: [Person] はwa [noun] ですdesu。

How to use

Use this pattern to say who someone is — their name, job, or role.

Replace [Person] with the subject and [noun] with their identity.

EXAMPLES

• わたしwatashiはwa がくせいgakuseiですdesu。
I am a student.

• あなたanataはwa せんせいsenseiですdesu。
You are a teacher.

• かれkare/かのじょkanojoはwa いしゃishaですdesu。
He/She is a doctor.

PATTERN 2: DESCRIPTION

"I am ___." / "She is from ___."

Pattern A: [Person] はwa [adjective] ですdesu。

Pattern B: [Person] はwa [place] しゅっしんshusshinですdesu。

How to use

Pattern A: Use this to describe how someone feels or what they are like. Replace [adjective] with a describing word.

Pattern B: Use this to say where someone is from. しゅっしんshusshin means "from (hometown/origin)."

EXAMPLES

• わたしwatashiはwa うれしいureshiiですdesu。
I am happy.

• かれらkareraはwa とてもtotemoねむいnemuiですdesu。
They are very sleepy.

• かのじょkanojoはwa かんこくKankokuしゅっしんshusshinですdesu。
She is from Korea.

Making Questions

To turn any ですdesu sentence into a question, just add かka at the end.

かのじょkanojoはwaせんせいsenseiですdesu。 → She is a teacher.

かのじょkanojoはwaせんせいsenseiですdesuかka？ → Is she a teacher?
