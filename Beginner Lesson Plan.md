Main Takeaways

1. Each **lesson outcome** should be **functional** (can do smth)  
2. Each **activity** should have a **goal**

**Class Fundamentals**

* Doesn’t matter if it is pattern based or grammar based \- But should be linked to CEFR functions and each lesson should clearly answer the question “what did I learn today?”  
* Linked to a topic *(e.g. What are you going to do this weekend?, Complaining about a hotel room., etc.)*  
* Each lesson should have a goal for payback *(e.g. “I can ask what someone is going to do in the future”, “I can complain if I don’t like something”, etc.)*  
* CLT is the core of the lesson \- Focussed on real immersion in the language \- The student should be learning by doing  
* Activities mimic real-life. \- All activities have a goal (e.g., Find a time to meet) \- this means the student focuses on the success of the communication rather than the fear of making a mistake.  
* Real-life situations \+ goal achieved \= immediate payback from the lesson

—----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

**Opening Page**

* Title of the lesson (the topic)  
* Grammar point/sentence structure to be studied

**Vocab & Grammar Activities**

* Vocab  
  * Vocab/Phrases \- With Korean translation  
  * Sentences containing selected pieces of vocab/phrases in Korean language \- Student should produce this sentence in English

* Grammar/Pattern  
  * Introduce the grammar/pattern for the class (e.g. They verb-ed TV yesterday.)  
  * Fill in the gap activity \- 4 Sentences in English (based on the grammar/pattern) with a gap in it \- Same sentence in the Korean language as a reference for the student \- e.g. (Do you \_\_\_\_\_\_\_ ? \- 농구 좋아하세요?)  
  * Word-order activity \- 4 sentences in English focused on the grammar/pattern but they are in the wrong word-order \- The student produces the correct sentence (e.g. watched they yesterday TV)  
  * Sentence expansion \- provide a broken sentence or some words as scaffolding, and the student expands on it using the grammar/pattern (e.g. for first conditional \-  "Rain / Stay home" —--\> If it rains, I will stay home.)

**CLT Activities (same activities at each level (A1, A2, B1) but with different difficulty)**

* Information Gap Activity \- One person has information the other lacks \- Communicate to find the information  
  * **A1 Level** \- The student and tutor have incomplete weekly planners. They must ask "When do you...?" or "Are you free on...?" to find a time to meet for coffee.  
  * **A2 level** \- The student and tutor each have a summer schedule with some missing information. They must ask questions using the future progressive, such as “What will you be doing in July?” or “Will you be traveling in August?”, to find out each other’s plans and complete their schedules. Once all the information is filled in, they decide when they will both be free to meet.  
  * **B1 level** \- The tutor has flight prices/times; the student has a specific budget and destination requirements. The student must interview the tutor to extract the best possible itinerary.

* Task-Based Activity \- Language instruction happens as a result of the struggle to complete the task  
  * **A1 Level**  
    * Task: The student must successfully call the tutor (acting as a pizza shop) and order a specific pizza with two toppings and give an address.  
    * AI Content: The AI generates a menu and a delivery address for the student.  
  * **A2 level**  
    * The Task: The student must plan a short weekend trip with the tutor and decide what they will be doing each day. The student asks questions and suggests activities using the future tense (e.g., “What will we do on Saturday morning?” or “Will we visit the museum in the afternoon?”). By the end of the activity, they must agree on a simple two-day plan.  
    * AI Content: AI generates a small list of possible activities (e.g., beach visit, museum trip, hiking, food market, city tour) along with a simple weekend schedule template with some time slots already filled and others empty. The student must ask questions and discuss options with the tutor to complete the plan.  
  * **B1 Level**  
    * Task: The student must interview the tutor about their weekly habits and routines using adverbs of frequency (e.g., always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never). After asking questions such as “How often do you exercise?” or “Do you usually cook at home?”, the student must identify one or two habits the tutor wants to improve and suggest a better weekly routine. By the end of the activity, they must agree on a short “improved routine” plan.  
    * AI Content: AI generates a weekly habits chart showing several activities (e.g., exercise, cooking, studying, watching TV, meeting friends) with some frequency information missing. The student must ask questions to complete the chart and then propose changes to create a healthier or more productive routine.

* Roleplay with Constraint \- roleplay but with rules and goals to be achieved  
  * **A1 Level**  
    * The Scenario: You are at a fruit market. The tutor is the vendor.  
    * Student’s Goal: Buy ingredients for a fruit salad.  
    * The Constraints: \* You only have $10.  
    * You must buy at least 3 different types of fruit.  
    * You cannot buy anything yellow (you hate the color).  
    * Focus: Numbers/Prices, "How much is...?", and basic preferences.  
  * **A2 Level**  
    * The Scenario: You just checked into a hotel room, but there are problems.  
    * Student’s Goal: Get the tutor (Hotel Manager) to give you a different room.  
    * The Constraints: \* You must complain about two things (e.g., the WiFi, the noise).  
    * You cannot raise your voice (you must be "polite but firm").  
    * Focus: "There is/There are," "I would like," and adjectives for problems (loud, broken, slow).  
  * **B1 Level**  
    * The Scenario: You are interviewing for a job as a "Social Media Manager."  
    * Student’s Goal: Convince the boss (the tutor) that you are the best candidate.  
    * The Constraints: \* You must mention a mistake you made in a past job (and how you fixed it).  
    * You cannot use the word "Good" or "Hard-working" to describe yourself (must use synonyms like efficient, creative, dedicated).  
    * Focus: Present Perfect (experiences), Past Simple, and professional vocabulary.

### **Sentence Production**

*Produce the English sentence based on the Korean prompt.*

1. 내일 비가 오면, 저는 우산을 가져갈 거예요. → (**If it rains tomorrow, I will take an umbrella.**)

### **Grammar/Pattern**

**Structure:** We use the First Conditional to talk about things that might happen in the future.

**Pattern:** If it \[Present Simple\], I will \[Verb\].

*Example: "If it is hot, I will go swimming."*

### **Fill in the Gap**

1. If the museum \_\_\_\_\_\_ (be) closed, we \_\_\_\_\_\_ (go) to the park. (박물관이 닫으면, 우리는 공원에 갈 거예요.)

3. If you \_\_\_\_\_\_ (forget) your umbrella, you \_\_\_\_\_\_ (get) wet. (우산을 잊어버리면, 당신은 젖을 거예요.)  
4. If it \_\_\_\_\_\_ (be) sunny, I \_\_\_\_\_\_ (wear) sunglasses. (날씨가 화창하면, 저는 선글라스를 쓸 거예요.)

* **AI Content:** A "Lifestyle Chart" with 5 categories: **Sleep, Fast Food, Exercise, Overtime, Stress**.  
* **Goal:** The student must suggest 3 changes using frequency adverbs. (e.g., "You *constantly* eat fast food. You should *occasionally* cook at home instead.")

### **3\. Roleplay with Constraint: "The Intervention"**

* **The Scenario:** Your best friend (the tutor) is a "workaholic." They are starting to feel **burnout**. You are trying to convince them to take a vacation.  
* **Student’s Goal:** Get the friend to agree to a 3-day trip.  
* **The Constraints:** \* Use the adverbs **"seldom"** and **"constantly"** at least once.  
  * You cannot use the word "**Work**."  
  * You must mention a "**Habit**" that is hurting their health.  
* **Focus:** Using frequency adverbs to highlight problematic patterns persuasively.

# **Beginner Japanese Lesson Structure**

### **Principles**

* Include **guides for both students and tutors in the textbook**, similar to textbooks now we are using in mylight and JLPT textbook.

* Maintain **repetition for learning effectiveness**, but increase the **variety of activities** to minimize boredom.

* Include **moderate challenge points** to keep students engaged.

  * Some questions asked by the tutor will **not on the preview materials**, encouraging students to respond spontaneously.

  * Provide students with the experience of **asking questions directly to the tutor**, instead of only passively answering the tutor’s questions.

* Include tools (such as a **simple translator**) to help students feel **safe and comfortable participating**, even if they do not fully understand everything.  
* Add a **tutor-only script page** so tutors can conduct lessons with **consistent quality and flow**.  
  * in native campus, tutors are using script in the class  

 

---

# **\[First Page\]**

The lesson should begin in a way that **reassures students who cannot yet understand Japanese**, while also ensuring that **tutors feel comfortable and not pressured**.

### **Tutor Script (just simple ver)**

Hello, \[Student Name\]\! Today let’s enjoy learning Japanese together.  
 Don’t worry — we’ll only use very simple Japanese.

Now, let’s read today’s two patterns together\!

(The tutor reads first, and the student repeats.)

These tutor lines should be written on the first page, and the tutor should **highlight or point to the text while speaking**.

Every part of the textbook should include:

* **Japanese guidance for tutors**  
* **Korean guidance for students**

![][image27]![][image28]

---

# **\[Patterns\]**

Introduce **two patterns per 1 textbook**.

* No grammar explanations  
* Only **very simple meanings**

Each pattern includes:

* **5 vocabulary words** that can be used together with the pattern.

Students will then create **two sentences using the pattern**.

Example format:

* Sentence 1  
* Sentence 2

The tutor should **write the sentences that the student says** in the provided space.

Additionally:

* Include **two simple tutor questions per pattern** that students can answer using the pattern.  
* These questions **are not on the preview materials**.

---

# **\[Dialogue & Translation part\]**

For each pattern:

* Include a **short dialogue (about 4 lines)**.

On the next page:

* The student’s line appears as a **blank space**.  
* The student **translate and complete the dialogue**.

---

# **\[Ask the Tutor\]**

Include **two questions that students can ask the tutor** using the target pattern.  
(not on the prestudy textbook)

The tutor will answer using the target pattern.

To control difficulty:

* Tutors can follow a **script provided on the tutor script page**.

Since some tutors may not follow the script:

* Tutors can also **write their answers in the chat**

Students can then **use a translator in our class page if necessary**, ensuring they do not feel anxious about completely failing to understand the tutor.

---

# **\[Wrap-up Page\]**

The goal is to **make the student feel like they completed an important mission**, encouraging them to continue to the next lesson.

### **Tutor Script (just simple ver)**

You did great today\! You did this well, and this too\!

(The tutor checks the two patterns learned today on the final page, presenting them as **missions completed**.)

Shall we read the key expressions one more time?

(The student reads the key expressions again).

Once the student finishes reading, the tutor should **give strong positive praise** and close the lesson.

---

# **Essential Features Required for This Lesson Format**

* The textbook should follow the **natural lesson flow**, allowing tutors to guide students who do not understand Japanese.  
* Clear **Japanese tutor guidance and Korean student guidance** must be included.  
* A **tutor-only script page**.  
* A **simple translation tool**.

---

**Optional but Recommended Features**

* A **Hanamaru (well-done) stamp** system for encouragement.
