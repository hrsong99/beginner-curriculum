PRESTUDY: これkoreはwaなんnanですdesuかka？

Things Around You — これkore・それsore・あれare

WHAT YOU WILL LEARN
In this lesson, you will learn how to:
• Point at things and say what they are
• Say "this," "that," and "that over there"
• Say who something belongs to
• Ask "What is this?" and "Whose is it?"

THE BIG IDEA: これkore・それsore・あれare

English Explanation

Japanese has three words for "this/that," based on distance from the speaker:

• これkore = this — near me (the speaker)

• それsore = that — near you (the listener)

• あれare = that over there — far from both of us

English vs. Japanese

English only has two: "this" (near) and "that" (far).

Japanese splits "that" into two — それsore (near the other person) and あれare (far from everyone).

PATTERN 1: DEMONSTRATIVES

"This is a ___."

Pattern: これkore/それsore/あれareはwa [noun] ですdesu。

How to use

Point at something and say what it is. Choose これkore, それsore, or あれare based on how far away it is.

EXAMPLES

• これkoreはwaほんhonですdesu。
This is a book.

• それsoreはwaペンpenですdesu。
That is a pen.

• あれareはwaかばんkabanですdesu。
That over there is a bag.

PATTERN 2: POSSESSION

"This is my ___."

Pattern: これkore/それsore/あれareはwa [person]のno [noun] ですdesu。

How to use

The particle のno shows that something belongs to someone. It works like "'s" in English.

わたしwatashiのnoほんhon = my book  (literally: "I 's book")

せんせいsenseiのnoペンpen = the teacher's pen

EXAMPLES

• これkoreはwaわたしwatashiのnoほんhonですdesu。
This is my book.

• それsoreはwaせんせいsenseiのnoペンpenですdesu。
That is the teacher's pen.

• あれareはwaゆきYukiさんsanのnoかばんkabanですdesu。
That over there is Yuki's bag.

ASKING QUESTIONS

QUESTION 1: "WHAT IS THIS?"

How to ask

Replace the noun with なんnan (what) and add かka at the end.

これkoreはwaほんhonですdesu。 → This is a book.

これkoreはwaなんnanですdesuかka？ → What is this?

• これkoreはwaなんnanですdesuかka？ What is this?
• それsoreはwaなんnanですdesuかka？ What is that?
• あれareはwaなんnanですdesuかka？ What is that over there?

QUESTION 2: "WHOSE IS THIS?"

How to ask

Use だれdare (who) + のno to ask "whose."

これkoreはwaだれdareのnoかばんkabanですdesuかka？ → Whose bag is this?

CONVERSATION PREVIEW

CONTEXT / じょうきょうjōkyō
Yuki and Alex are in a classroom looking at things on the desk.

How to read this

Read through the conversation slowly. The words in italics are the patterns you are learning. English translations are below each line.

YUKI: アレックスArekkusuさんsan、これkoreはwaなんnanですdesuかka？
Alex, what is this?

ALEX: ああaa、それsoreはwaわたしwatashiのnoほんhonですdesu。
Oh, that is my book.

YUKI: いいiiほんhonですdesuねne！これkoreもmoほんhonですdesuかka？

ALEX: いいえiie、それsoreはwaノートnōtoですdesu。せんせいsenseiのnoノートnōtoですdesu。
No, that is a notebook. It's the teacher's notebook.

YUKI: あa、そうsōですdesuかka。あれareはwaなんnanですdesuかka？
Oh, I see. What is that over there?

ALEX: あれareはwaサラSaraさんsanのnoかばんkabanですdesu。とてもtotemoおおきいōkiiですdesuねne！
That over there is Sara's bag. It's very big!
